# Marketing and Sales Automation

For New Zealand SMEs, marketing often feels like a Catch-22: you need to market to grow, but you're too busy running the business to market effectively. AI is changing this equation, enabling small teams to execute sophisticated marketing strategies that previously required dedicated specialists or expensive agencies.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Identify AI applications across the marketing and sales funnel
- Select appropriate AI marketing tools for different business needs
- Implement AI-powered content creation responsibly
- Use AI for customer segmentation and personalisation
- Measure the effectiveness of AI marketing initiatives

## The Marketing Challenge for Kiwi SMEs

New Zealand's SME owners typically wear multiple hats. Marketing competes for attention with operations, finance, HR, and customer service. The result? Marketing often becomes reactive rather than strategic—posting on social media when there's time, sending newsletters sporadically, and missing opportunities for systematic growth.

AI addresses this by:
- Reducing time needed for content creation
- Automating repetitive marketing tasks
- Providing insights previously requiring data analysts
- Enabling personalisation at scale
- Maintaining consistency when owners are busy

## AI Across the Marketing Funnel

### Awareness Stage: Content Creation and Distribution

**AI-Powered Content Creation**

Generative AI has revolutionised content production for SMEs. Tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Jasper can help create:
- Blog posts and articles
- Social media content
- Email newsletters
- Website copy
- Product descriptions
- Video scripts

A Hamilton boutique owner can generate a month's worth of social media posts in an afternoon. A Tauranga accountant can produce educational blog content without spending days writing.

> **Warning:** AI-generated content requires human review and editing. Don't publish raw AI output—it often lacks personality, may contain errors, and can sound generic. Use AI to create first drafts, then add your expertise and voice.

**Smart Content Distribution**

AI tools analyse when your audience is most active and optimise posting schedules. Platforms like Buffer, Hootsuite, and Later use AI to recommend optimal posting times for different social networks.

**AI Image Generation**

Tools like Canva's AI features, DALL-E, and Midjourney create custom images for marketing. This is particularly valuable for SMEs without design budgets:
- Social media graphics
- Blog post images
- Product mockups
- Ad creative variations

### Consideration Stage: Lead Nurturing and Email Marketing

**Email Marketing Automation**

AI enhances email marketing through:

*Personalised Subject Lines:* AI analyses what drives opens for your audience and generates subject line variations.

*Send Time Optimisation:* Rather than blasting everyone at 9am, AI sends emails when individual recipients are most likely to open them.

*Content Personalisation:* Dynamic content blocks change based on recipient characteristics—showing different products to different segments.

*Predictive Lead Scoring:* AI identifies which leads are most likely to convert, helping you focus follow-up efforts.

Popular platforms with AI capabilities include:
- **Mailchimp**: Built-in AI features for subject lines, send times, and content suggestions
- **Klaviyo**: Strong AI for e-commerce email and SMS
- **HubSpot**: Comprehensive marketing automation with AI features
- **ActiveCampaign**: AI-powered automation and predictive sending

**Chatbot Lead Qualification**

Website chatbots can qualify leads before they reach your sales team:
- Gathering initial information
- Understanding needs and budget
- Scheduling appropriate meetings
- Routing enquiries to the right person

### Decision Stage: Sales Enablement

**CRM Intelligence**

Modern CRMs incorporate AI to support sales:
- Predicting deal close probability
- Recommending next best actions
- Identifying at-risk deals
- Suggesting optimal pricing

**AI Sales Assistants**

Tools like Gong and Chorus analyse sales calls, providing insights on:
- What messaging resonates with prospects
- Where conversations go off track
- How top performers differ from average
- Competitor mentions and positioning

While these tools are more common in larger organisations, some are becoming accessible for SMEs.

### Retention Stage: Customer Intelligence

**Churn Prediction**

AI can identify customers likely to leave before they do, based on:
- Engagement patterns
- Purchase frequency changes
- Support ticket sentiment
- Website behaviour

**Customer Segmentation**

AI clusters customers into meaningful segments based on behaviour, enabling:
- Targeted retention offers
- Personalised communication
- Appropriate upsell recommendations
- Efficient resource allocation

## Practical AI Marketing Tools for NZ SMEs

### Content and Copywriting

**ChatGPT Plus (~$30 NZD/month)**
The most versatile option for content creation. Use it for:
- First drafts of any written content
- Brainstorming ideas and angles
- Repurposing content for different channels
- Research and summarisation

**Claude (~$30 NZD/month)**
Similar capabilities to ChatGPT with particular strength in longer-form content and analysis.

**Canva Pro (~$20 NZD/month)**
Includes AI features for:
- Image generation
- Background removal
- Design suggestions
- Content resizing for different platforms

### Email Marketing

**Mailchimp (Free tier available, paid from ~$20 NZD/month)**
Good for SMEs starting with email marketing. AI features include:
- Subject line helper
- Send time optimisation
- Content recommendations
- Customer journey builder

### Social Media

**Buffer (~$15 NZD/month)**
AI-powered scheduling and analytics. Suggests optimal posting times and analyses performance patterns.

**Lately (Various pricing)**
Uses AI to learn your brand voice and generate social content from longer-form content like blog posts or podcasts.

### Analytics and Insights

**Google Analytics 4 (Free)**
Now includes AI-powered insights that automatically surface important trends and anomalies in your website data.

**Hotjar (Free tier available)**
AI analyses user recordings and heatmaps to identify UX issues and conversion opportunities.

## Case Study: AI Marketing for a Wellington Food Producer

**Kāpiti Artisan Foods** (fictional composite example)

A Wellington-based producer of specialty food products wanted to expand their direct-to-consumer online sales without hiring a marketing team.

**AI Tools Implemented:**
1. ChatGPT for content creation
2. Canva Pro for visual assets
3. Mailchimp for email marketing
4. Buffer for social media scheduling

**Results Over 6 Months:**
- Blog content: From 0 to 2 posts per month (AI-assisted drafts, human editing)
- Social media: Consistent daily posting (previously sporadic)
- Email: Weekly newsletter with 28% open rate (industry average: 21%)
- Time spent on marketing: 5 hours/week (down from sporadic 8-10 hours)
- Online sales: 45% increase

**Key Insights:**
- AI reduced content creation time by ~70%
- Human oversight remained essential for brand voice and accuracy
- Automation freed mental energy for strategic thinking
- Initial setup took ~2 weeks of learning and configuration

## Best Practices for AI Marketing

### Maintain Authenticity

AI should enhance your marketing, not replace your voice. Tips:
- Use AI for first drafts, then inject personality
- Include personal anecdotes and NZ-specific references
- Review all content before publishing
- Don't over-automate customer interactions

### Be Transparent

If customers might be interacting with AI, be honest about it. Trust is essential for NZ SMEs competing against faceless multinationals.

### Ensure Accuracy

AI can generate plausible-sounding nonsense. Verify:
- Facts and statistics
- Product specifications
- Legal or compliance claims
- Pricing and availability

### Respect Privacy

When using AI for personalisation:
- Follow Privacy Act 2020 requirements
- Be transparent about data use
- Provide opt-out options
- Secure customer data appropriately

### Measure and Iterate

Track what matters:
- Content engagement (not just production volume)
- Lead quality (not just quantity)
- Conversion rates
- Customer feedback
- ROI on AI tool investments

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Over-Reliance on AI
AI is a tool, not a strategy. Poor marketing automated is still poor marketing. Focus on strategy first, then use AI to execute efficiently.

### Publishing Without Review
AI makes mistakes and produces generic content. Always review and enhance AI output before publishing.

### Ignoring Brand Voice
AI-generated content can sound the same as everyone else's AI content. Differentiate by adding your unique perspective and voice.

### Automating Everything
Some marketing moments deserve personal attention. A loyal customer's anniversary, a significant deal, a complaint—these need human touch.

### Tool Overload
It's easy to sign up for many AI tools. Focus on mastering a few that address your biggest needs rather than spreading thin.

## Key Takeaways

- AI enables SMEs to execute sophisticated marketing without dedicated teams
- Applications span the entire marketing funnel from awareness through retention
- Numerous affordable tools exist at SME-friendly price points
- Human oversight remains essential for quality, accuracy, and brand voice
- Start with high-impact, low-complexity applications and expand gradually
- Measure results and iterate based on what actually works
